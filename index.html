Shawn Tenbrink

Author & U.S. Diplomat

Biography

For over a decade, Shawn Tenbrink has served on the front lines of U.S. diplomacy, navigating the volatile intersections of geopolitics, conflict, and international commerce. A career Foreign Service Officer with the U.S. Department of State, Tenbrink’s global perspective was shaped early on; he was raised in Indonesia and maintains deep roots in his father’s hometown of Cincinnati, Ohio.

His work is anchored by a robust academic foundation, including a B.A. in History from Miami University of Ohio and a Master of Laws (LL.M.) in International Economic Law from the University of Kent’s Brussels School of International Studies.

Tenbrink’s professional journey began in the Moroccan Sahara as a Peace Corps volunteer. Living among the historic "mud castles" of the High Atlas Mountains, he practiced the "people-to-people" diplomacy that remains the hallmark of his worldview. This early immersion in cross-cultural negotiation prepared him for a rigorous career in some of the world’s most challenging environments.

His diplomatic portfolio is defined by its scale and intensity. During the onset of the Arab Spring, he served in Damascus, Syria—a professional crucible that provided the primary source material for his first book. His subsequent assignments spanned the Middle East and North Africa, including tours in Oman, Morocco, and two critical assignments in Lebanon. Beyond the region, he served in Sydney, Australia, where he met his wife, Tammy.

Before his current posting, Tenbrink served as a Senior Advisor to the U.S. Special Envoy to Monitor and Combat Antisemitism, tackling this critical issue on a global stage. Throughout his career, he has been recognized with numerous accolades from the Department of State, including multiple Superior Honor and Meritorious Honor Awards.

Currently stationed at the Foreign Press Center in New York City, Tenbrink dedicates his time outside the office to chronicling the visceral, often overlooked intersections of diplomacy. His historical non-fiction work, Diplomatic Blood for Trade, meticulously reconstructs the life and assassination of Henry Heusken, the first U.S. diplomat killed in the line of duty.
